Common Cold and Influenza

The common cold and influenza (flu) are both respiratory illnesses but caused by different viruses. While they share similar symptoms, the flu is typically more severe.

Common Cold:
Causes:
- Most commonly caused by rhinoviruses
- Over 200 different viruses can cause colds
- Spread through airborne droplets and direct contact

Symptoms:
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Sore throat
- Cough
- Sneezing
- Mild headache
- Mild body aches
- Low-grade fever (rare)
- Generally mild and gradual onset

Duration: Usually 7-10 days

Treatment:
- Rest and fluids
- Over-the-counter cold medications for symptom relief
- Throat lozenges
- Saline nasal drops
- Pain relievers (acetaminophen or ibuprofen)
- No antibiotics needed (viral infection)

Influenza (Flu):
Causes:
- Caused by influenza viruses (types A, B, C)
- More serious than common cold
- Seasonal epidemics typically in winter

Symptoms:
- Sudden onset of high fever (100°F-104°F)
- Severe body aches and muscle pain
- Extreme fatigue
- Dry cough
- Sore throat
- Headache
- Chills
- Sometimes nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (more common in children)

Duration: Usually 1-2 weeks, but fatigue can last longer

Treatment:
- Rest and plenty of fluids
- Antiviral medications (if started within 48 hours of symptom onset)
  - Oseltamivir (Tamiflu)
  - Zanamivir (Relenza)
- Pain relievers and fever reducers
- Over-the-counter medications for symptom relief

When to See a Doctor:
Common Cold:
- Symptoms lasting more than 10 days
- High fever
- Severe sinus pain
- Symptoms in children under 3 months

Influenza:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Chest pain or pressure
- Sudden dizziness or confusion
- Severe or persistent vomiting
- Symptoms that improve then return with fever and worse cough
- High-risk groups (elderly, pregnant, chronic conditions)

Prevention:
Common Cold:
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid touching face
- Avoid close contact with sick people
- Disinfect surfaces

Influenza:
- Annual flu vaccine
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid close contact with sick people
- Cover coughs and sneezes
- Stay home when sick
- Disinfect surfaces

Complications:
Common Cold: Usually minimal
- Secondary bacterial infections (sinusitis, ear infections)

Influenza:
- Pneumonia (bacterial or viral)
- Bronchitis
- Sinus infections
- Ear infections
- Worsening of chronic medical conditions
- Myocarditis (heart inflammation)
- Encephalitis (brain inflammation)
